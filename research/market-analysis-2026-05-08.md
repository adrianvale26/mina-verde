## Key Market Observations

1. **Cert premium is real:** GIA-certified Colombian emeralds command noticeably higher prices than those with commercial lab certs or no cert. A GIA F1/Minor 1.36ct in platinum + diamonds was ~$8,292; comparable 1.5ct 14K gold pieces without GIA were ~$5,266 (discounted from ~$7,500).

2. **Treatment disclosure is standard at top retailers:** Leibish, 1stDibs, and JR Colombian Emeralds all explicitly disclose treatment levels. F2/moderate enhancement is a known and accepted category in this market.

3. **Metal choice matters:** 14K gold pendants (no diamonds) in the 1–2 Ct range appear to price $2,500–$6,500. Adding platinum or accent diamonds pushes prices to $7,000–$12,000+.

4. **Finished pendant vs. loose stone:** The closest data for finished 18K gold pendants with ~1.4–1.5 Ct Colombian emeralds points to a **$2,500–$6,000 retail range**, with most clustering around $3,500–$5,500 depending on design complexity and cert status.

5. **eBay price anchors low:** eBay tends to list lower, which can pull buyer expectations down — but 1stDibs and JR Colombian Emeralds confirm the premium market remains healthy at the $4,000–$8,000 level for well-certified pieces.

## Positioning + Pricing Recommendation

**For a 1.40 Ct oval Colombian emerald, GIA certified, F2/moderate enhancement, set in 18Kt yellow gold pendant (cost basis: $1,300):**

### Competitive Positioning

- **Leibish loose stone data:** A comparable 1.40 Ct GIA-adjacent (GRS) stone alone is $7,100. A 1.41 Ct with commercial cert (CD) is $4,894. Stone-only benchmarks in this size bracket: **$4,500–$7,500**.
- **1stDibs finished pendant data:** 1.36 Ct GIA F1/Minor in platinum + diamonds: ~$8,292. 1.5 Ct in 14K gold + halo: ~$5,266 (discounted). No-diamond solitaire 18K gold pieces in this size range: estimated **$3,000–$5,000**.
- **JR Colombian Emeralds:** 2.51 Ct in 14K white gold: $6,500 (larger stone pushes price up, but 2.5 Ct is ~2.5x the weight). 2.99 Ct in 18K: $5,700. Extrapolating down to 1.40 Ct: **$2,500–$4,500**.

### Recommended Retail Price

| Scenario | Retail Price | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Conservative** (design-forward, not luxury) | $2,800–$3,200 | Undercut market to build volume; 2.1–2.5x cost basis |
| **Market-rate** (fair, competitive) | $3,500–$4,500 | In line with JR Colombian Emeralds and 1stDibs solitaire prices |
| **Premium** (GIA certification + fine design) | $4,500–$5,500 | Leverage GIA cert as a trust signal; appeal to buyer willingness to pay for provenance |

### Recommended Positioning

**$3,800–$4,200 for the finished 18K gold pendant** represents the optimal point:
- It is **below** the comparable 1stDibs GIA pieces ($5,000–$8,000) while matching cert quality — positioning as "best value GIA pendant under $5,000"
- It is **above** un-certified eBay/rubylane pieces ($1,500–$3,000) — justified by GIA origin confirmation
- At 2.9–3.2x cost basis ($1,300), the margin is healthy for a sub-$5,000 piece
- It anchors to the higher-priced Leibish loose stone market ($7,100 for 1.40 Ct GRS) — customers perceive they are getting a deal on the stone + getting the setting included

**Marketing angle:** "GIA-Certified 1.40 Ct Colombian Emerald Pendant — $3,999. The stone alone would cost $7,100 elsewhere."
