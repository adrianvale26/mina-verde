Cert Transparency Notes

F2 / Moderate enhancement is a known and accepted category in the fine emerald market. Major platforms (Leibish, 1stDibs, JR Colombian Emeralds) all disclose treatment levels explicitly. F2 sits between Minor Oil and Fracture Filling — it is not a concern if disclosed, which this GIA report does. Buyers working at this price point understand the treatment scale.

GIA is the strictest major lab — an origin-confirmed Colombian GIA report is a strong trust signal that commercial labs (GRS, CD, IGI) cannot replicate. This is a genuine competitive advantage in positioning.

Key Market Observations

  1. Cert premium is real. GIA-certified Colombian emeralds command noticeably higher prices than commercial lab equivalents. A GIA F1/Minor 1.36ct in platinum+diamonds: ~$8,292. Comparable 1.5ct 14K without GIA: ~$5,266 discounted.
  2. Treatment is disclosed across all major platforms. F2/moderate enhancement is standard market category. Full transparency with GIA report is an asset, not a liability.
  3. Metal choice drives price meaningfully. 14K gold solitaire, no diamonds, 1–2ct: $2,500–$6,500. Platinum or accent diamonds push to $7,000–$12,000+.
  4. Finished pendant vs. loose stone matters. The closest finished 18K gold pendant benchmarks suggest a $2,500–$6,000 retail range, clustering around $3,500–$5,500 depending on cert and design.
